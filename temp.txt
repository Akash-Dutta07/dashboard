One final note

Earlier we used expose: for internal communication because it's a useful way to illustrate which services are intended to be internal. In modern Docker Compose, services on the same Compose network can usually communicate by service name even if you omit expose: entirely. The key idea is that only the services that should accept traffic from outside the VPS need ports:, while internal services (database, Redis, and often the backend when behind Nginx) stay on Docker's private network. That's the principle to remember, regardless of the exact Compose syntax you choose.
